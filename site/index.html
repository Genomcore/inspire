A software engineering framework for the agentic era

Software that breathes.

INSPIRE is a way to build software with AI agents. When generating code is cheap, the scarce act is no longer writing it — it's knowing what to build, and telling whether it's right.

Breathe in

The shift

AI changed software development. Our frameworks haven't.

For decades, software development was a coordination problem: getting teams of humans aligned around implementation. Agile was built for that world.

Now the unit of work is changing. A single individual can orchestrate a swarm of AI agents, shifting the bottleneck from coordination to judgment.

The agile era

SCRUM

Built for teams of humans coordinating over weeks. Work is split into time-boxed Sprints, with roles, estimation and daily stand-ups — because the bottleneck was collaboration between people.

The agentic era

INSPIRE

Built for one person orchestrating a swarm of AI agents in a single session — a Breath. Guardrails, skills, QA and version control replace the ceremonies; the bottleneck is judgment, not coordination.

The principles

Seven letters. Seven breaths.

INSPIRE is not an acronym stretched over a process. It is seven properties that allow a software product and its underlying knowledge to emerge, evolve and remain coherent in the agentic era.

I

Individual

The human becomes the orchestrator.

Software no longer emerges only from teams of humans coordinating implementation. A single contributor, amplified by AI agents, can now direct complex systems through intent, context and judgment.

N

Navigable

Context remains discoverable.

A navigable system connects intent, specifications, decisions, implementation and verification into a coherent knowledge graph. Humans and agents can discover context, trace decisions and evolve the system without losing understanding.

S

Spec-driven

Intent becomes the source of truth.

The specification captures what the system should be, while code becomes an expression of that intent. Implementation can change, but the knowledge behind it remains durable, explicit and verifiable.

P

Prototypical

Explore before committing.

Prototypes are not early versions of the product; they are disposable instruments for learning. They make uncertainty visible, allowing ideas to evolve before becoming part of the persistent knowledge layer.

I

Iterative

Progress comes from refinement.

The first version is not expected to be right — it is expected to reveal what is missing. Each iteration reduces uncertainty, revisits previous decisions and moves the system closer to what it should become.

R

Regenerative

Intent survives implementation.

The specification is the DNA of the system; code is its current expression. When implementation drifts or becomes constrained, agents can regenerate, refactor and reshape it while preserving the original intent.

E

Enforceable

Coherence is protected by design.

Intent alone is not enough. Guardrails, skills and automated checks turn principles into constraints that guide agents and keep every change aligned with the system's standards.

The unit of work

Not sprints. Breaths.

In the agentic era, the unit of work is no longer time — it is context. INSPIRE works in Breaths: one intent, one context, taken exactly as deep as it needs.

Inhale — internalize

Take the context and go deep. The human builds the understanding needed to decide what should exist — the one place where scarce judgment enters the loop.

Exhale — externalize

Materialize that understanding into durable artifacts — an architectural decision, a specification, an implementation change. The breath lands as a pull request.

A Breath has no fixed size. It is defined by context and impact — sometimes a sweeping change, sometimes a single line of code.

Two speeds

The system breathes differently as it matures.

The system breathes at the pace of its responsibilities.

Young System — Growing through intent

Accelerated breathing

The system breathes with force and intent. It can change direction quickly because its history is still light.

Mature System — Evolving through memory

Conscious breathing

The system breathes with depth and experience. It evolves through the memory it has accumulated, preserving what gives it identity.

The boundary isn't the release date. It's the moment the system acquires history.

The spiral

The spiral of convergence.

The phases of development aren't stages you pass once, nor a flat loop you repeat. Every Breath reduces uncertainty, increases value and moves the product closer to release.

Discover · Intent, Architecture, Design

Take in intent and shape the form. A wide, shallow prototype answers: is this the right thing?

Specify · Contracts, Schemas, Interfaces

Turn intent into concrete contracts. A narrow, deep spike answers: can we build it as we think?

Generate · Implementation

Agents produce the software from the specification, with a bounded margin. The product becomes real.

Verify · Tests, QA, Reality

Tests derived from the spec catch drift; human testing validates what the spec cannot express.

A Breath is not a cycle. It is another turn of the spiral.

Prototyping

Prototypes create clarity.

No prototype is the product. Its purpose is to reduce uncertainty and refine the persistent knowledge layer. Different prototypes answer different questions before that knowledge shapes the evolution of the product.

One horizontal prototype

wide · shallow · not functional

A rough but navigable representation of the whole surface — mocked but real. Cheap enough that showing the intended form beats drawing a mockup of it.

Is this the right thing?

Many vertical prototypes

narrow · deep · fully functional

One focused spike per module, section or component — each running end-to-end in its own isolated sandbox, exercising real behaviour proving that the specification can become reality.

Can we build it as we think?

The knowledge base

Knowledge lives in a navigable graph.

The specification is not a document. It is a connected knowledge graph.

Every durable decision, specification and piece of knowledge is a plain-text artifact connected into one navigable graph. It becomes the product's memory — a source of truth that humans and agents can read, write and reason over, long after any implementation has been regenerated.

The graph is the shared context between humans and agents.

Enforceable

Coherence is protected by design.

When agents move this fast, coherence can't rely on human discipline or implicit memory. INSPIRE makes it structural — through enforcement and trusted foundations.

Driving the Process

The guardrails

Skills, hooks, linters and QA turn the specification's rules into automated checks. Every change is validated against standards before it lands — drift is caught mechanically, not remembered.
